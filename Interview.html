💻 Computer Science – Top 15 Questions

❓ What is OOP?
💡 Object-Oriented Programming organizes code into objects containing data and methods, promoting reuse and modularity.
❓ Explain polymorphism.
💡 Polymorphism allows one interface with multiple implementations, such as method overloading and overriding.
❓ What is a database index?
💡 An index improves data retrieval speed by creating quick lookup references in a database table.
❓ Define normalization in DBMS.
💡 Normalization organizes data to reduce redundancy and improve consistency using normal forms.
❓ Difference between TCP and UDP?
💡 TCP is reliable, connection-oriented, and slower. UDP is faster, connectionless, but less reliable.
❓ What is an operating system?
💡 An OS manages hardware, software resources, and provides services for programs.
❓ Explain deadlock.
💡 Deadlock occurs when processes are stuck waiting for each other’s resources indefinitely.
❓ What is a thread?
💡 A thread is the smallest unit of execution within a process, enabling multitasking.
❓ Explain cloud computing.
💡 Cloud computing delivers computing services like storage and servers over the internet.
❓ What is machine learning?
💡 ML is a subset of AI where systems learn from data patterns and improve performance automatically.
❓ What is Big O notation?
💡 Big O describes algorithm efficiency in terms of time and space complexity.
❓ Define compiler vs interpreter.
💡 A compiler translates code before execution; an interpreter translates line-by-line during execution.
❓ What is recursion?
💡 Recursion is when a function calls itself until a base condition is met.
❓ Explain stack vs queue.
💡 Stack follows LIFO (Last In First Out), queue follows FIFO (First In First Out).
❓ What is an API?
💡 An API is an interface that allows different software systems to communicate and share data.

⚛️ Physics – Top 15 Questions

❓ What is Newton’s first law?
💡 A body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.
❓ Define work in physics.
💡 Work is done when a force is applied on an object and it displaces in the direction of force.
❓ What is kinetic energy?
💡 The energy possessed by a body due to its motion, given by ½mv².
❓ Explain potential energy.
💡 The stored energy in a body due to its position or configuration, e.g., mgh.
❓ What is Ohm’s Law?
💡 Voltage across a conductor is directly proportional to current (V=IR).
❓ Define power.
💡 Power is the rate of doing work, P = Work/Time or P = VI in electricity.
❓ What is refraction?
💡 The bending of light when it passes from one medium to another of different density.
❓ Explain Doppler effect.
💡 The apparent change in frequency of a wave due to relative motion of source and observer.
❓ What are longitudinal waves?
💡 Waves in which particles vibrate parallel to the wave direction, e.g., sound waves.
❓ What is gravitational constant (G)?
💡 G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ Nm²/kg², the universal constant of gravitation.
❓ Define acceleration.
💡 The rate of change of velocity with respect to time.
❓ What is centripetal force?
💡 The force that keeps an object moving in a circular path, directed toward the center.
❓ What is the speed of light?
💡 The speed of light in vacuum is 3×10⁸ m/s.
❓ Explain nuclear fission.
💡 A heavy nucleus splits into smaller nuclei with release of huge energy, e.g., atomic bomb.
❓ Explain nuclear fusion.
💡 Two light nuclei fuse to form a heavier nucleus with large energy release, e.g., Sun.

🧫 Microbiology – Top 15 Questions

❓ What are bacteria?
💡 Bacteria are single-celled prokaryotic microorganisms found in diverse environments.
❓ Define virus.
💡 Viruses are acellular infectious agents that require a host to replicate.
❓ What is Gram staining?
💡 A differential staining technique used to classify bacteria as Gram-positive or Gram-negative.
❓ Define pathogen.
💡 A pathogen is a microorganism that causes disease in its host.
❓ What are fungi?
💡 Fungi are eukaryotic organisms that include yeasts, molds, and mushrooms.
❓ What are protozoa?
💡 Protozoa are single-celled eukaryotic organisms, often motile and heterotrophic.
❓ Explain antibiotic resistance.
💡 It is when bacteria evolve mechanisms to resist the effects of antibiotics.
❓ What is sterilization?
💡 The complete elimination of all microorganisms, including spores, from an object.
❓ Define aseptic technique.
💡 Procedures used to prevent contamination by unwanted microorganisms.
❓ What are prions?
💡 Misfolded proteins that can cause neurodegenerative diseases, e.g., mad cow disease.
❓ What is a culture medium?
💡 A nutrient solution or solid used to grow microorganisms in the lab.
❓ What is the difference between aerobic and anaerobic bacteria?
💡 Aerobic bacteria need oxygen; anaerobic bacteria grow without oxygen.
❓ What is a biofilm?
💡 A structured community of microorganisms encased in a protective matrix attached to surfaces.
❓ Define normal flora.
💡 Microorganisms that naturally live in or on the human body without causing disease.
❓ What is a vaccine?
💡 A biological preparation that provides immunity to a specific disease.

🧪 Chemistry – Top 15 Questions

❓ What is an atom?
💡 The smallest unit of matter that retains the properties of an element.
❓ Define molecule.
💡 A group of two or more atoms bonded together chemically.
❓ What is the periodic table?
💡 A tabular arrangement of elements based on atomic number and properties.
❓ Define covalent bond.
💡 A chemical bond formed when two atoms share electrons.
❓ What is ionic bond?
💡 A bond formed by the transfer of electrons from one atom to another.
❓ Define pH.
💡 A scale that measures hydrogen ion concentration; acidity or alkalinity.
❓ What is an acid?
💡 A substance that donates protons (H⁺ ions) in a solution.
❓ What is a base?
💡 A substance that accepts protons or releases hydroxide ions (OH⁻).
❓ What is electrolysis?
💡 A process of decomposing compounds using an electric current.
❓ Define oxidation.
💡 Loss of electrons or increase in oxidation state of an element.
❓ Define reduction.
💡 Gain of electrons or decrease in oxidation state of an element.
❓ What is Avogadro’s number?
💡 6.022 × 10²³, the number of particles in one mole of a substance.
❓ Define molarity.
💡 The number of moles of solute per liter of solution.
❓ What are isotopes?
💡 Atoms of the same element with the same atomic number but different mass numbers.
❓ Define catalyst.
💡 A substance that speeds up a reaction without being consumed in it.
